Hineini tells Yahoo's Fire Eagle service about your location via GPS or the nearest cell tower, or with the address you manually type into the textbox at the top of the screen.

If you manually type an address, you must click the 'Update' button afterwards. A message on the screen will tell you if Fire Eagle can't locate the address you've typed in.

Your update interval will automatically switch to "Manually" when you type text into the textbox.  Clicking 'Update' with text in the textbox will clear the textbox after a successful update.  Clicking 'Update' with no text in the textbox will immediately start an update regardless of your Update Interval setting. 

When you Update (manually or automatically), you're sending a location (address, cell tower, or GPS coordinates) to Fire Eagle.  But Fire Eagle might not record you as being at that exact location.  Rather, it knows about a vast and finite number of locations, and it records you as being at whichever of those locations is nearest the location it received.

Typically, that will be a street address, or maybe just the name of a nearby city or town.  The location's name and map will appear on the screen, with the following notations:

  • * means you gave your location
  • + means GPS gave your location
  • ~ means a cell tower gave your location
If there's a problem, you can check the Error Log for details.
